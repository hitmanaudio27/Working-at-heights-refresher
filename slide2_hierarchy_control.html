EBTA Prevention-First Philosophy

The hierarchy of control prioritizes elimination of hazards at the source, working down to less effective controls only when higher-level measures are not reasonably practicable.

EBTA's approach emphasizes Safety in Design (SiD) to implement Level 1 and 2 controls during project planning and design phases.

Level 1: Elimination

Remove the hazard entirely through design solutions and prefabrication techniques.

Level 2: Substitution

Replace high-risk work methods with safer alternatives like MEWPs and rope access.

Level 3: Isolation

Separate workers from hazards using physical barriers and protective systems.

Level 4: Engineering

Implement engineered solutions like scaffolding and mechanical work platforms.

Level 5: Administrative/PPE

Use procedures, training, and personal protective equipment as the last line of defense.
